What is BMI ?

BMI stands for Body Mass Index. It is a value derived from an individual's weight and height and is commonly used as a screening tool to indicate whether a person is underweight, overweight, or has a healthy weight.

Where is it used ?

  • Clinical Settings: Used by healthcare professionals for routine health assessments.
  • Public Health Initiatives: Analyzed to monitor population-level trends in obesity.
  • Fitness Centers: Employed by trainers to assess clients' weight status.
  • Research Studies: Utilized to investigate the relationship between weight and health outcomes.

How it is calculated ?

The BMI (Body Mass Index) is calculated using the formula:

\[ \text{BMI} = \frac{\text{weight (kg)}}{\text{height (m)}^2} \]
